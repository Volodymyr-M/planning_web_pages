Main Concepts Last updated: 2023-02-11

Tasks

While planning your project you enter Durations as estimates, meaning that the duration is a reasonable guess of how much a task will take. Estimating a task is a science on itself, you might have enough data to estimate a task from your previous experience (like “it took 4 hours last time, so this time is should take approximately the same”) or might need to get some help from other people who have experience doing or estimating the task.

Once a task has a work resource assigned (like a person who does the task), Work property of the task becomes different from 0. It shows the time all resources would spend working on the task. If there are 2 people working on a task of duration of 1 day, Work becomes equal to 16 hours.

Task Dependencies

We've learned that linking 2 tasks makes the second task start after the first one finishes. Such link is called "Finish-To-Start" dependency, and is one of 4 dependency types available: "Finish-To-Finish" (the second task finishes at the same time as the first task finishes), "Start-To-Finish" (the second task finishes at the same time as the first task starts), "Start-To-Start" (the second task starts at the same time as the first task starts).

Once there's a dependency set between tasks (in other words, the tasks are linked), one of them is called predecessor of the other.

"Finish-To-Start" dependency is the most widely used dependency, that's why it's possible to set it by linking tasks. Dependencies of other types can be set in "Task Properties" dialog, "Predecessors" tab.

Resources

As you know, a project always requires some resources to complete. Some of these resources are work resources: people and equipment, they do tasks thus contributing to the project. Some of these resources are material resources: materials which are used in the project. For example, in construction project, a building is created by people with the help of some machinery (people are work resources, machinery are also work resources), and the building is created of bricks and concrete (material resources).

For many projects is important to track who performs which task and which materials are used in the project. [Project] helps you to do this by allowing you to add the resources and assign resources to tasks.

A work resource can be overallocated, which means that it has more work assigned than it can do based on its calendar data. For example, if you have 2 tasks with duration of 1 day with no dependency between them both assigned to the same work resource, the resource is overallocated: in that 1 day the resource has to do 2 days of work! To resolve this, in this example simply linking the tasks fixes the issue.

To add a resource, switch to "Resources" view by tapping on "Resources" button in the sidebar. Just like with tasks, you add resources by tapping "+" button.

Calendars

[Project] has 3 predefined calendars, one of which (Standard) is used by default. Two others (Night shift and 24-hours) exist for your convenience in case you need to quickly assign one of these calendars to your resources or tasks.

Calendars define days/time when work can be done. For example, as mentioned above, Standard calendar defines, that work is done Monday-Friday 8-5 with 1 hour break. This means that [Project] will not put tasks which use this calendar on Saturday, Sunday or at night.

By default, [Project] uses "Standard" calendar for everything in your project. This means that your resources work and your tasks are done 8 hours a day 8 to 5 (with 1 hour break), 5 days a week (Monday-Friday). But in real projects some of this may be incorrect. For example, it's not uncommon that one of your team members works part-time, another one has a vacation in the middle of your project, and your whole company has non-working days due to national holidays.

To reflect this information in your schedule you need to adjust calendars (the default one for holidays or resource calendars for vacations and personal work times).
